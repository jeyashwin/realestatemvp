Why live off campus?

1. Live Near the Goods, Services, and Amenities you want: By choosing to live off campus, you can choose to live near grocery stores, supermarkets, or the night life in your area.
2. Less-Expensive: Whether you want to live in an apartment by yourself, or in a house with a few others, off-campus housing will save you money!
3. Car Parking: If you want to have your car with you, it is likely that you will not have the opportunity to do so right away when you live on campus.
4. Privacy: Living off-campus gives you a high level and sense of privacy that you will likely not see when living on-campus.

Dorm Rates vs. Apartment Rent

Apartment living is almost always significantly cheaper than dorming.

Dorm (SBU Numbers) Off-Campus (Based on Averages)
$9,800 - $11,000 p/year $6,000 - $8,000 p/year
$4,900 - $5,500 p/semester $3,000 - $4,000 p/semester
$1,225 - $1,375 p/month $750 - $1,000 p/month

The rents above are rough averages based on location and amenity preferences of previous student renters like yourself.

Lease Signing

After agreeing a price with a landlord for a particular home or apartment, the next step after approval is signing your lease. Generally there are 2 ways to sign the lease:

1. In-Person: Paper copy signed by tenants and notarized
2. Online: Signed by tenants and landlord through an online platform (we allow files to be sent through our messaging platform)
